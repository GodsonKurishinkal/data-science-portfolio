Project Overview

Building on the demand forecasting foundation, this comprehensive inventory optimization system answers critical supply chain questions: How much inventory should each store hold? When should we reorder? Which items need the most attention? By combining ABC/XYZ analysis, safety stock optimization, EOQ calculations, and cost modeling, this system provides actionable recommendations that balance cost efficiency with customer service.

Business Challenge

Retail businesses lose $1.77 trillion annually due to overstocking and understocking. Excess inventory ties up capital and incurs 15-25% annual holding costs, while stockouts lead to lost sales and customer dissatisfaction. This system optimizes inventory levels to minimize total costs while maintaining high service levels.

Key Features

ABC/XYZ Analysis

Multi-dimensional classification based on revenue contribution (ABC) and demand variability (XYZ) for targeted inventory strategies.

Safety Stock Optimization

Risk-based buffer inventory calculation using service level targets, demand variability, and lead time uncertainty.

Reorder Point Calculation

Intelligent reorder triggers that account for lead time demand and safety stock requirements to prevent stockouts.

Economic Order Quantity

Cost-minimizing order quantities that balance ordering costs against holding costs for optimal inventory levels.

Multi-location Optimization

Store-level inventory allocation considering local demand patterns, lead times, and service level requirements.

Comprehensive Cost Modeling

Total cost analysis including holding costs, ordering costs, and stockout costs for data-driven decision making.

Key Insight

AX items (5% of inventory) generate 40% of revenue with predictable demand, requiring continuous monitoring and highest service levels. In contrast, CZ items (10% of inventory) contribute only 0.1% of revenue with erratic demand, making them prime candidates for discontinuation or on-demand ordering.

Key Findings

  • AX items require 3x higher safety stock than CX items but generate 40x more revenue
  • Lead time variability has 2x more impact on safety stock than demand variability
  • EOQ optimization reduced ordering frequency by 30% while maintaining service levels
  • Seasonal products benefit from dynamic safety stock that adjusts with demand patterns
  • Multi-location pooling can reduce total safety stock by 15-25% compared to independent stores
  • Automated reordering eliminated 95% of manual stock level checks
